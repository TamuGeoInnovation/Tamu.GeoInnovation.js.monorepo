Census Intersection

Associate census variables with latitude and longitude data

Turn Points Into Knowledge

The absolutely most simple thing that you can do is intersect your geocoded output with Census tract boundaries to figure out which Census tract the geocoded location is located within. Extremely simple, but yet the basis for most research that uses geocoded data.

Using this service, you can get back Census tract, block, and block group information from the 1990, 2000, and/or 2010 US Census. These data allow you to link your points with socio-demographic variables from the Census as well as other administrative data such as voting and school districts.
