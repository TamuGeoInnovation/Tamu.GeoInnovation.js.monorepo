Crowd-Sourced COVID-19 Lockdown and Testing Sites

As part of Texas A&M's commitment to assist the health care community and general public, this website is an effort to collect and maintain an accurate and up-to-date database of county lockdowns and testing centers/facilities. These collected data are part of a larger series of services provided to facilitate information dissemination.

Anybody is free and encouraged to submit new testing sites as they become available, or update existing information. All submissions are reviewed by a committee of qualified professionals to ensure the quality of data.

Purpose

The Texas A&M GeoInnovation Service Center is undertaking several efforts to provide the resources and information needed by professionals and the public.

Collection

Volunteered geographic information (VGI) can be difficult to collect and maintain. Texas A&M GeoInnovation Service Center seeks to establish a standardized reporting format for information on counties, lockdowns, and testing sites.

 all_out

Dissemination

Collected crowd-sourced data is used to create visualizations and maps to facilitate the consumption and dissemination of accurate data. In addition, all data is also offered to the general public through API's for use in other applications.

Resources

Several other free resources for professionals and analysts actively working in COVID-19 fighting efforts are available. These include geographic data sets as well as unlimited use of our Geocoding services.

Free Geocoding For COVID-19 Efforts

Texas A&M Geoservices is fully prepared to continue supporting service operations during during the coronavirus COVID-19 disease pandemic. In addition, the Texas A&M University (TAMU) GeoInnovation Service Center has been authorized by TAMU to provide assistance to the national and international public health communities by providing free and unlimited access to our geocoding platform for COVID-19 public health-related surveillance, treatment, and control in the United States.
