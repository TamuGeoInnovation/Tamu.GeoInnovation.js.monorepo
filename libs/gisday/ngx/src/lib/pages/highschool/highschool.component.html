What is GIS Day?

GIS stands for Geographic Information Systems. GIS uses location technology to answer questions, make better decisions, and solve some of the worldâ€™s toughest problems. It is the Science of Where.

GIS Day is an international celebration of GIS technology. Around the globe, GIS professionals dedicate time to sharing their knowledge and accomplishments with others.

GIS Day event planners, from around Texas, have joined forces to host the first state-wide GIS Day for Texas. TxGIS Day is a free, virtual event for high school students and teachers. Our goal is to introduce GIS to high school students and help you inspire future GeoGeeks!

High School Opportunities

Behind-the-Scenes Look

Registered attendees will have access to a selection of videos showing GIS professionals in their work environment. Get a behind-the-scenes look at the offices of:

  • Aerial Robotics, the official drone operator of Texas
  • Metro
  • Texas Emergency Response Team
  • Gulf Interstate
  • Quadrant Consultants

College and University Recruiting

Registered attendees will have opportunties to connect with Faculty, Academic Advisors, and current and former students from several colleges and universities including:

  • Houston Community College
  • Texas A&M University
  • The University of North Texas
  • The University of Texas at Austin

Discussion Panels

Registered attendees will have access to a selection of discussion panels that include GIS professionals. Attendees can listen in as GIS professionals tell their stories and discuss how meaningful their work has contributed to:

  • Coronavirus Response
  • Drones
  • Emergency Response
  • Engineering
  • Law Enforcement
  • U.S. Military

Games & Prizes

Students can register to join a live geography based game and compete for prizes.

Seats will be limited!

GIS Essay Contest

Students can submit an essay about GIS and compete for an award and prizes. Because this is a state-wide event we ask teachers to limit the essay submissions to three (3) per school. Each essay will be reviewed by a panel of GIS professionals. Click here for more details.

GIS Labs

Students can register for a seat in one of our GIS Labs. We are offering live GIS Labs where the students can enjoy an interactive experience using GIS technology.

Seats will be limited!

GeoMentor Panel for Teachers

The AAG- Esri ConnectED GeoMentor Program is working to enhance GIS and geography education in US K-12 schools through the introduction of GIS into classrooms across the country. It connects teachers to the GIS world. Join a live discussion panel featuring GIS software representatives, GeoMentors, and K-12 school teachers as they discuss offerings and success stories.

Seats will be limited!
