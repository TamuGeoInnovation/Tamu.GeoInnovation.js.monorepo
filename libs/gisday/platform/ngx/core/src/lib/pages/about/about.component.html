About TxGIS Day

GIS Day, the annual worldwide salute to geospatial technology and its power to transform and enhance lives, is going to be extra "spatial" this year in Texas, where the TxGIS Day celebration, one of the world's largest, is expanding to encompass three event-packed days, November 14-16, 2022.

Why You Should Become an Event Volunteer

  • Gain experience running a technical conference
  • Build your resume
  • Make connections with attendees and peers

Responsibilities of Event Volunteers

  • Help check-in visitors and attendees
  • Count event session attendees
  • Setup/test event video/audio
  • Provide event technical assistance

Why You Should Become a Student Ambassador

  • Gain experience running a large technical conference
  • Build your resume
  • Make one-on-one connections with industry professionals

Responsibilities of Student Ambassadors

  • Assigned to support the visit of one or more external guests from industry
  • Keep in contact with their industry guest(s)
  • Help assigned industry guest(s) with logistics - parking, getting to/fro event venues
  • Help assigned industry guest(s) feel welcome and part of the event

GIS Resume Bank

The TxGIS Day Committee has unique and personal relationships with many employers in the fields of Geography, Geographic Information Science & Technology (GIST), and the broader Geospatial Industry. These employers are actively seeking to recruit undergraduate, graduate, and former students from Texas A&M University, The University of Texas at Austin, the University of North Texas, and many other schools and colleges to fill full time and internship roles.

In 2018, the TxGIS Day Committee organized the first TxGIS Day Resume Bank to make sure that our current and former students receive the highest number of resume reviews by the highest number of potential employers. This resource is provided to companies and organizations who request access to TxGIS Day's best GIS students from across the state.

Participation

Any current or former student at a participating TxGIS Day college or univeristy is eligible to add her or his resume to our GIS Resume Bank.

Participation in this initiative is voluntary on the part of our students. No student will be required to add her or his resume to the TxGIS Day Resume Bank as a condition for graduation or grades in a course. A student's optional contribution of her or his resume into the GIS Resume Bank will be used to help her/him/them find an internship or full time position. Accordingly, any information voluntarily provided by a student into this system will be shared with potential employers on their behalf as requested by employers.

Student Resume Inclusion Criteria

Quality standards will apply. If your resume does not reflect well on yourself or TAMU, it will not be included in the TAMU GIS Resume Bank. You may or may not be notified if this is the case, depending on the volume of resumes submitted.

Current and former students may resubmit their resume as many times as they would like as new experience, skills, etc. are obtained and their resume gets better and better. Only the latest submission will be shared with potential employers.

Student Resume Submission Guidelines

Current and former students who wish to add their resume to the GIS Resume Bank should:

  • Read the TxGIS Day Resume Bank Instructions
  • Submit their resume to the TxGIS Day Resume Bank

Employer Access Information

Potential employers may request access to the TxGIS Day Resume Bank by using our Contact Form to send us a message.
